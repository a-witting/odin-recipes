Pasta Fagioli

This homemade pasta fagioli soup is a simple, comforting dish made with wholesome pantry and fresh ingredients. It features tender ditalini pasta, creamy Great Northern beans, and sliced zucchini in a savory vegetable broth base. The soup is seasoned with dried basil, oregano, and a touch of garlic for a warm, herby flavor. Diced tomatoes add depth, while a drizzle of olive oil ties everything together. Served with garlic-seasoned croutons for a bit of crunch, this easy, hearty soup is perfect for a cozy lunch or light dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 (14oz) cans vegetable broth
  • 1 (15oz) can Great Northern Beans - drained and rinsed
  • 1 (14oz) can Diced tomatoes
  • 2 zucchini quartered lengthwise and sliced
  • 1 tbs olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp minced garlic
  • 1/2 tsp dried basel
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 c uncooked tubetti or small shell pasta
  • 1/2 c seasoned croutons
  • 1/2 c grated Asiago or Romano cheese

Directions

  1. Combine broth, beans, tomatoes, zucchini, oil, garlic, basil, and oregano in CROCK-POT slow cooker; mix well. Cover; cook on LOW 3 to 4 hours.
  2. Stir in pasta. Cover, cook on LOW 1 hour or until pasta is tender. Serve soup with croutons and cheese.
